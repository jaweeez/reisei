# Reisei — Voice & Positioning

**冷静 · Stay level. Composed.** A coach, not a counselor.

## What Reisei actually is

Reisei is men's mental health in a discipline costume. The real work is emotional: noticing what you're feeling before it runs you, staying present in your body when something spikes, and working it through instead of burying it. Most men were handed two settings, shut it down or blow up. Reisei trains the range in between.

The costume matters. Discipline-minded men bounce a wellness app on sight, so we lead with the standard, the streak, the line. But the weight of the product is the emotional and somatic practice underneath. Keep the costume, weight the substance.

## Say it this way (not that)

The concepts are the point now. The substance IS what you're feeling, your body, the present moment. What repels this audience is not the ideas, it's the clinical framing and the soft cliches. Say the human version.

| Instead of (clinical / soft) | Say (Reisei) |
|---|---|
| your emotions, process your feelings | what you're feeling, what's actually going on, what's under it |
| anxiety, anxious | wired, keyed up, restless, when it spikes |
| depression, depressed | flat, heavy, can't get going |
| mindfulness, meditate, be present | get present, back in your body, notice it, Reset |
| emotional regulation, self-soothe | name it, feel where it sits, let it move |
| trauma, triggered | what set it off, the thing that hooks you |
| somatic, body scan | where it sits in your body, drop into your chest |
| self-care, wellness, mental health (as a label) | never label it, just do the work |

Still never ship: pamphlet cliches ("you've got this", "proud of you", "amazing", "you're not alone"), clinical jargon of any kind, emoji, hype exclamation marks, and **em dashes** (use periods, commas, colons, or parentheses). If a string could appear on a therapist's website, rewrite it. If it sounds like a drill sergeant, soften it.

## The translation table

Every mechanic is one real practice wearing a discipline costume. The costume is the name, the substance is the emotional and somatic work it does.

| What it really is | How we frame it | The work underneath |
|---|---|---|
| Daily emotional check-in | **The Line** (an honest read on where you're at) | self-monitoring, naming what's here |
| Behavioral activation | streak, "one day at a time" | showing up when you don't feel like it |
| Cognitive reframing | **the coach**, dichotomy of control | catching the story you're telling yourself |
| Emotion regulation | **Stay level** | feel it fully, don't hand it the wheel |
| Reflection | **the log** (call it a log) | putting words to it |
| Grounding, breathing | **Reset** (60s to get back in your body) | somatic regulation when it spikes |
| Perspective, values | **The Bearing** (a daily principle from a tradition you pick) | meaning, distance from the noise |
| Coping-ahead | premeditation | deciding your move before the moment |
| Social support | **your Corner** (people who actually know how you're doing) | not doing it alone, no performing |
| Incremental change | kaizen, raise the bar | 1% sharper |

## Three levers (why it lands on this audience)

1. **Identity before behavior.** They won't "work on their mental health", but they'll "hold a line", an identity they want. Once they're someone who holds the line, getting present or naming a feeling is congruent with that identity, not a betrayal of it.
2. **Status makes honesty safe.** The RESPECT ack (for an honest miss) makes vulnerability high-status inside the Corner, the one thing a therapist can't manufacture. Protect this mechanic, it's load-bearing.
3. **Pointed at the self.** The Corner keeps you honest when no one's watching. It's a witness, not a scoreboard against friends.

## The kaizen escalation ladder

Earn the right to introduce each practice. Never front-load the emotional work, let the discipline carry them in.

- **Weeks 1 to 2. Hook.** One line, check in daily, your Corner sees it. Reads as pure discipline.
- **Weeks 3 to 4. Reset.** The coach offers a Reset on hard days. Get out of your head, back in your body. *(shipped)*
- **Month 2. The Bearing.** A daily principle from a tradition you pick, aimed at what's actually coming up. *(shipped)*
- **Month 2 and on. The log.** Put words to it, private.
- **Ongoing. Premeditation.** "Name where today knocks you off. Decide your move now."

## Copy examples (in voice)

- Held day: *"Logged. You showed up today."*
- Honest miss: *"You logged it honestly. That counts."*
- Missed a day: *"Yesterday got away from you. No story to tell yourself, just check in today."*
- Reset invite: *"Head's not right? 60 seconds. Get back in your body, then check in."*
- Coach, grounded (anger): *"That's anger talking, not the situation. Name it, one breath, then move from level."*
- The Bearing (a state, a tradition, a technique): *"Restless today. The Stoics would say you're renting space to things you don't control. Try this: write down the one thing that's actually yours to move, and start there."*

## The one guardrail (strengthened)

The content now touches real feelings: anger, grief, shame, urges. That raises the duty of care. Keep it genuinely helpful, and keep a real off-ramp. If someone reads like they're in a genuinely dark place, do not coach it, and do not perform care we're not equipped for. Say plainly, in voice, that this isn't the tool for right now, and point to a real resource (a crisis line, a real person). The Trojan horse is legitimate. Masquerading as care we're not is not.
