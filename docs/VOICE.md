# Reisei — Voice & Positioning

**冷静 · Stay level.** A coach, not a counselor. Direction, not mood.

## The strategy: translate, don't disguise

Reisei delivers evidence-based mental-health practice to an audience that would
*bounce* a wellness app on sight — discipline-minded people who want to hold themselves
to a higher standard (Stoicism, mindfulness, kaizen). We don't hide the medicine; we
**remove the cultural tax** on it. The same practice that reads as "soft" in clinical
language reads as *aspirational* in a discipline idiom. That's the whole product.

## Banned words (never ship these)

`mental health` · `wellness` · `self-care` · `mood` · `feelings` · `anxiety` ·
`depression` · `journaling` · `meditation` · `mindfulness` (as a label) · `therapy` ·
`healing` · `you've got this` · `amazing` · `proud of you` · emoji · exclamation marks
(except a single hard milestone).

If a string could appear in a hospital pamphlet, rewrite it.

## The translation table

Every mechanic is one evidence-based practice wearing a discipline costume.

| The benefit (what it really is) | Repels them | Reisei framing |
|---|---|---|
| Self-monitoring / daily check-in | "mood tracking" | **The Line** — did you hold your standard today |
| Behavioral activation | "take a small step" | streak + **"an honest break beats going dark"** |
| Cognitive reframing (CBT) | "challenge negative thoughts" | **Dichotomy of control** (the coach) |
| Emotional regulation | "manage your anxiety" | **Stay level. Cold head.** |
| Reflection / journaling | "journaling" | **Field report / after-action review** |
| Mindfulness / breathing | "meditation" | **Reset** — 60s to get your head right |
| Gratitude / perspective | "gratitude journal" | **View from above / memento mori** |
| Coping-ahead / relapse prevention | "trigger plan" | **Premeditatio malorum** |
| Social support (anti-loneliness) | "support group" | **The crew** — the witness |
| Incremental change | "small steps" | **Kaizen** — 1% sharper, raise the bar |

## Three levers (why it lands on this audience)

1. **Identity before behavior.** They won't "track their mental health"; they'll "hold
   a line" — an identity they *want*. Once they're "someone who holds the line," a Reset
   is congruent with that identity, not a betrayal of it. Front-load discipline; back-load
   the softer practices.
2. **Status makes honesty safe.** The **RESPECT** ack (for an honest break) makes
   vulnerability *high-status inside the crew* — the one thing a therapist can't
   manufacture. Protect this mechanic; it's load-bearing.
3. **A higher standard than those around you** — pointed at the *self*. The crew is the
   witness that keeps you honest when no one's watching, not a scoreboard against friends.

## The kaizen escalation ladder

Earn the right to introduce each practice; never front-load.

- **Weeks 1–2 — Hook.** One line, log daily, crew witnesses. Pure discipline.
- **Weeks 3–4 — Reset.** The coach offers a Reset on break/dark days. *(shipped)*
- **Month 2 — Debrief.** A weekly after-action review (what held, what broke, where it clusters).
- **Month 2+ — Raise the bar.** Kaizen nudge: sharpen the line, or add a second.
- **Ongoing — Premeditation.** "Name where today knocks you off. Decide your move now."

## Copy examples (in voice)

- Held day: *"Held. The line holds."*
- Honest break: *"Broke. Logged honestly — integrity stands."*
- Dark day (morning after): *"Missed yesterday. The line reset. Stand it back up today."*
- Reset invite: *"Head's not right? 60 seconds. Then log."*
- Coach, grounded: *"That belief is the trap, not the drink. Log today, restart at 1, move."*

## The one guardrail

We *actually* help people, so keep it genuinely helpful and keep a real off-ramp — a
quiet line to a real resource for someone in a dark place, always in voice, never
clinical theater. The Trojan horse is legitimate; masquerading as care we're not isn't.
